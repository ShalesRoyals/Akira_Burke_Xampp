Cornwall

Cornwall is the westernmost of the three historic counties into which Jamaica is divided. It includes Montego Bay , the islands's second largest city by area. There is a total of five parishes in Cornwall. They are Hanover, Saint Elizabeth, Saint James, Trelawny and Westmoreland.

Middlesex

Middlesex is the central of the three historic counties into which Jamaica is divided. Middlesex is considered the largest area and population of the three counties. It includes Spanish Town, the island's original capital. There is a total of five parishes in Middlesex. They are Clarendon, Manchester, Saint Ann, Saint Catherine and Saint Mary.

Surrey

Surrey is the easternmost and the smallest by area of the three historic counties into which Jamaica is divided. It was created in 1758, and is divided into four parishes. They are Saint Thomas, Portland , Kingston (capital of Jamiaca) and Saint Andrew.

The Importance of Finding the Right College. Is college worth it?

When you pick a college, it's a lot like picking a home. You need to like the environment you're in, enjoy the people around you, and be able to live or stay there for the next few years of your life. While most prospective students overlook this part of the college process, it is one of the most vital parts to successfully finding a college that suits you.

Benefits of Going to College. How will I benefit from going to college?

In today's economy, people are beginning to wonder about the benefits of going to college and earning a college degree. It is true that in recent years college graduates are having a harder time finding employment. No one wants to be jobless for the rest of their life. Three benefits of going to college are: A college graduate is likely to earn more than a non-grad. Millennials with college degrees have lower unemployment rate and poverty rates than those without degrees. And lastly college graduates are likely to find more satisfaction with their jobs than a non-graduate.
